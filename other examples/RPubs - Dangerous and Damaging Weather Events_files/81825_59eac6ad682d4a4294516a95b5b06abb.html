Dangerous and Damaging Weather Events

Lisa Anderson

May 23, 2015

Summary

A review of the NOAA Storm Database, which contains data concerning weather events between 1950 and 2011, reveals the following relevant points concerning dangerous and damaging weather events:

  • The most fatalities and injuries are caused by tornados
  • The most expensive property damage is caused by floods
  • The most expensive crop damage is caused by drought

The database contains a number of fields which are not necessary to this analysis and are removed. Additionally,the values in PROPDMG and CROPDMG must be transformed by the multiplier contained in PROPDMGEXP and CROPDMGEXP to obtain the full value of the damage, as follows:

  • H or h = hundred
  • K or k = thousand
  • M or m = million
  • B or b = billion
Sdata <- data[,c(2,8,23:28)]
Sdata$tempPropDmg <- Sdata$PROPDMG
Sdata$PROPDMG <-1*Sdata$tempPropDmg
Sdata$PROPDMG[grepl("[Hh]",Sdata$PROPDMGEXP)] <- 100*Sdata$tempPropDmg[grepl("[Hh]",Sdata$PROPDMGEXP)]
Sdata$PROPDMG[grepl("[Kk]",Sdata$PROPDMGEXP)] <- 1000*Sdata$tempPropDmg[grepl("[Kk]",Sdata$PROPDMGEXP)]
Sdata$PROPDMG[grepl("[Mm]",Sdata$PROPDMGEXP)] <- 1000000*Sdata$tempPropDmg[grepl("[Mm]",Sdata$PROPDMGEXP)]
Sdata$PROPDMG[grepl("[Bb]",Sdata$PROPDMGEXP)] <- 1000000000*Sdata$tempPropDmg[grepl("[Bb]",Sdata$PROPDMGEXP)]

Sdata$tempCropDmg <- Sdata$CROPDMG
Sdata$CROPDMG <-1*Sdata$tempCropDmg
Sdata$CROPDMG[grepl("[Hh]",Sdata$CROPDMGEXP)] <- 100*Sdata$tempCropDmg[grepl("[Hh]",Sdata$CROPDMGEXP)]
Sdata$CROPDMG[grepl("[Kk]",Sdata$CROPDMGEXP)] <- 1000*Sdata$tempCropDmg[grepl("[Kk]",Sdata$CROPDMGEXP)]
Sdata$CROPDMG[grepl("[Mm]",Sdata$CROPDMGEXP)] <- 1000000*Sdata$tempCropDmg[grepl("[Mm]",Sdata$CROPDMGEXP)]
Sdata$CROPDMG[grepl("[Bb]",Sdata$CROPDMGEXP)] <- 1000000000*Sdata$tempCropDmg[grepl("[Bb]",Sdata$CROPDMGEXP)]

Results

Most Dangerous Weather Events

The columns FATALITIES and INJURIES contain the observations we require to determine the most dangerous weather events. An initial aggregation of fatalities and injuries reveal that there are 985 unique weather events recorded in the database. When we review the weather event factors we observe duplications (based solely on uppper/lower case) and other potential duplications based on meaning. We choose to restrict our observations to weather events which caused at least 100 injuries in the history of the database.

Most Damaging Weather Events

The columns PROPDMG and CROPDMG contain the observations we require to determine the most damaging weather events. Due to the number of unique weather event factors mentioned above, we choose to restrict our observations to weather events which caused at least $100 Million worth of property damage in the history of the database.

bypropdmg <- aggregate(PROPDMG ~ EVTYPE, Sdata, sum)
bypropdmg <- bypropdmg[bypropdmg$PROPDMG > 100000000,]
bycropdmg <- aggregate(CROPDMG ~ EVTYPE, Sdata, sum)

The following plot illustrates property and crop damage by type of weather event.

costData <- merge(x=bypropdmg, y=bycropdmg, all.x=TRUE)
cd1 <- costData[,c(1,2)]
cd1$Damage <- "Property"
names(cd1) <- c("Event.Type", "Number", "Type.of.Damage")
cd2 <- costData[,c(1,3)]
cd2$Damage <- "Crop"
names(cd2) <- c("Event.Type", "Number", "Type.of.Damage")
costData <- rbind(cd1,cd2)

c1 <- ggplot(costData, aes(x=Event.Type, y=Number/1000000, fill=Type.of.Damage)) + 
  geom_bar(stat="identity") + theme(axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 90, size = 7)) +
  ylab("Millions of Dollars")
c1

The plot above shows that floods cause the most property damage, while drought causes the most crop damage.
